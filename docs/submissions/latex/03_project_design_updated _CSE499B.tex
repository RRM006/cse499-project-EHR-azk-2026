\section{Introduction}
%% ================================================================

This chapter presents the \textbf{updated} design of the \textit{AI
Medical Pre-Screening Assistant}. The original CSE499A design was a
five-stage one-shot pipeline: Speech $\to$ ASR $\to$ NER $\to$
Structured EHR $\to$ Doctor Dashboard. Based on empirical findings
from Phases~2 and~3 and clinical NLP requirements, the design has
been expanded to \textbf{15 functional modules} that add an adaptive
conversational follow-up loop, emergency detection, risk
classification, explainable AI, and a continuous feedback and
retraining pipeline. The system remains strictly a
\textit{pre-screening and decision-support tool}; final clinical
authority rests with the attending physician.

\subsection{Module Descriptions}
%% ================================================================

{\small
\begin{description}[leftmargin=0pt,labelwidth=0pt,style=nextline,
                    itemsep=1.5pt,parsep=0pt]

\item[\textbf{M1 --- Speech-to-Text.}]
Bangla/Banglish/dialect audio; VAD-segmented to 10--15\,s clips.
Best: \textit{BengaliAI Regional} (46.94\% WER); CSE499B target
$<$20\% WER via LoRA on Qwen3-ASR-1.7B.

\item[\textbf{M2 --- Text Processing \& Normalisation.}]
Spelling correction, Banglish normalisation, filler removal,
punctuation restoration, sentence-boundary detection.

\item[\textbf{M3 --- Information Extraction (NER).}]
BanglaBERT labels: \textit{symptom, site, duration, severity,
frequency, age, comorbidity, medication}; temporal expressions
normalised to standard intervals.

\item[\textbf{M4 --- Initial Clinical Summary.}]
Human-readable chief-complaint summary, e.g.\ \textit{``Fever + dry
cough 3 days; mild fatigue; no chest pain.''} Shown on dashboard.

\item[\textbf{M5 --- Emergency Detection} \textcolor{cred}{(new).}]
Rule-based checker + AI classifier. Red flags: chest pain, stroke
signs, severe dyspnoea, loss of consciousness, acute trauma.
Escalates immediately; pipeline continues in parallel.

\item[\textbf{M6 --- Missing Information Analysis} \textcolor{cred}{(new).}]
Per-symptom clinical checklist: \CheckedBox~Fever detected;
\XBox~Duration missing; \XBox~Temperature not provided.

\item[\textbf{M7 --- Follow-up Question Generation} \textcolor{corange}{(new).}]
Priority-ranked questions in Bangla or English. Example:
\textit{``Apnar jwor ki matro kal theke?''} /
\textit{``When did the fever start?''}

\item[\textbf{M8 --- Response Processing \& Profile Update} \textcolor{corange}{(new).}]
Patient answers re-enter M2--M3; extracted entities merged into
profile with conflict resolution; filled gaps marked.

\item[\textbf{M9 --- Case Completion Check} \textcolor{corange}{(new).}]
Scores completeness; if incomplete loops to M7; if complete (or max
turns) proceeds to M10.

\item[\textbf{M10 --- Risk Assessment} \textcolor{cred}{(new).}]
\textbf{Low / Medium / High / Critical} via clinical rule engine
weighted by age, comorbidities, severity, duration. Integrates M5.

\item[\textbf{M11 --- Explainable AI (XAI)} \textcolor{cred}{(new).}]
SHAP/LIME in plain language. Example: \textit{``High risk: fever
$>$5 days + dyspnoea + age $>$65.''}

\item[\textbf{M12 --- Clinical Report Generation} \textcolor{cred}{(new).}]
8-section report: Patient Profile, Chief Complaint, Symptoms,
Emergency Flags, Q\&A Log, Risk+XAI, Condition Categories, Next Steps.

\item[\textbf{M13 --- EHR Database.}]
HIPAA/PDPA-compliant PostgreSQL; encryption at rest and in transit;
full audit logging.

\item[\textbf{M14 --- Doctor Dashboard} (extended).]
Report, risk, flags, XAI. Annotation/override of any field (versioned);
real-time alerts for High/Critical.

\item[\textbf{M15 --- Feedback \& Continuous Learning} \textcolor{cgreen}{(new).}]
Doctors rate reports, correct extractions, annotate risk disagreements;
periodic retraining of M3, M7, M10 with regression testing.

\end{description}}

\section{CSE499A Implementation Phases}
%% ================================================================

{\small\begin{description}[leftmargin=0pt,style=nextline,
                            itemsep=2pt,parsep=0pt]

\item[\textbf{Phase 1 --- Audio Collection \& Preprocessing}]
\textit{Demo: 12 March 2026.}
Multi-dialect corpus (Dhakaiya, Barishali, Sylheti, Normal, Indian
Bangla); $\approx$4.7\,h, 10 sources, $\sim$1{,}100 clips after
WhisperX VAD segmentation at 16\,kHz mono PCM.

\item[\textbf{Phase 2 --- Baseline ASR Benchmark (12 models)}]
\textit{Demo: 2 April 2026.}
Best: \textbf{BengaliAI Regional} --- 46.94\% WER, 24.29\% CER,
0.273 BLEU. Key finding: dialect speech defeats all open Bangla models.

\item[\textbf{Phase 3 --- Large Multimodal LLM Benchmark}]
\textit{Demo: 9 April 2026.}
Qwen2-Audio, Qwen3-ASR-1.7B, Voxtral-Mini, Phi-4, Qwen2.5-Omni.
None matched BengaliAI Regional ($>$50 pp WER gap).
Conclusion: \textit{specialisation beats scale for Bangla dialect ASR}.

\item[\textbf{Phase 4 --- Fine-Tuning Methodology Study}]
\textit{Demo: 16 April 2026.}
LoRA adapter plan for Qwen3-ASR-1.7B: adapter on speech encoder,
decoder frozen. Training scheduled for CSE499B.

\section{Conclusion}
%% ================================================================

The 15-module design addresses every limitation of the original
five-stage pipeline: the adaptive follow-up loop (M6--M9) guarantees
clinical information completeness; M5 adds an emergency escalation
path; M10--M11 add risk stratification and explainability; M15 closes
the quality loop through physician-driven retraining. The CSE499B
priority is LoRA fine-tuning of Qwen3-ASR-1.7B to reach $<$20\%
dialect WER; Modules~3--15 will then be integrated iteratively.
